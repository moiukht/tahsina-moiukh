Research Experience

Analyzing Political Bias in Media Discourse: A Computational Approach Using NLP to Examine Israel-Palestine Coverage

Natural Language Processing, Computational Social Science, Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning

In this undergraduate thesis, I co-developed a computational framework to assess political bias in media discourse, which focused on coverage of the Israel-Palestine conflict. This work also involved creating a labeled dataset with seven bias categories. My contributions included annotating the unlabeled dataset with my two co-authors to create a high-quality labeled dataset, employing the GPT-3 API for data augmentation to address class imbalance, and co-implementing the pipeline for model training and evaluation. To work with the limited computing resources, I fine-tuned state-of-the-art models, including RoBERTa and LLaMA, using QLoRA to optimize the pre-trained models. We achieved an F1 score of 77.4% in a seven-class classification task. A key aspect of the research involved a thorough error analysis of false positives and false negatives across all classes which helped to determine the model‚Äôs shortcomings and potential areas for development. Currently, we are reviewing the manuscript of our work for publication.
